Criminal Law

The Criminal Code of the Republic of Goddard outlines the offenses that are punishable by law, as well as the penalties for those offenses. It also sets out the procedures for criminal investigations and trials.

  1. Murder, manslaughter, and attempted murder shall be punishable by life imprisonment or the death penalty, at the discretion of the court.
  2. Robbery, burglary, and theft shall be punishable by imprisonment for up to ten years, depending on the severity of the offense.
  3. Fraud, embezzlement, and forgery shall be punishable by imprisonment for up to five years, depending on the severity of the offense.
  4. Assault and battery shall be punishable by imprisonment for up to two years, depending on the severity of the injury inflicted.
  5. Drug possession, trafficking, and distribution shall be punishable by imprisonment for up to five years, depending on the quantity and type of drugs involved.
  6. Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs shall be punishable by imprisonment for up to one year, depending on the severity of the offense.
  7. All criminal investigations shall be conducted by the police or other law enforcement agencies, and shall be carried out in accordance with the laws governing search and seizure.
  8. All criminal trials shall be conducted by a judge or jury, and shall be based on the principle of innocent until proven guilty.
  9. The accused shall have the right to counsel and the right to a fair and speedy trial.
  10. The penalties for criminal offenses shall be determined by the court, taking into account the severity of the offense and the circumstances of the case.
